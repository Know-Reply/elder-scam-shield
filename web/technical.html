Technical Deep Dive

Architecture, research foundations, benchmark data, and signal taxonomy for Elder Shield's multi-agent scam protection pipeline.

1. Conversation Knowledge Graph

Most fraud detection systems ask: "is this message a scam?" Elder Shield asks: "who knows what, and how did they learn it?"

The conversation knowledge graph tracks every fact across both sides of the dialogue through three layers, drawing on recent advances in epistemic logic and dialogue-level relation extraction.

Layer 1: Fact Ledger (Signal)

Append-only per turn. Every entity (name, location, institution, amount) is tagged with who stated it first and whether the other party echoed it. This is information provenance at the conversational turn level — a capability that does not exist in production fraud detection systems.

Turn 0 (elder):  "Kenji, is that you?"
  → name:健二  first_stated_by: elder

Turn 1 (sender): "Yes, it's Kenji! I'm in Osaka."
  → name:健二  echo_detected: true, echo_by: sender
  → location:大阪  first_stated_by: sender (independent knowledge)

The sender didn't know Kenji's name. The elder gave it to them. Every subsequent use of "Kenji" by the sender is echoed knowledge, not proof of identity.

Layer 2: Epistemic State (Interpretation)

The elder's psychological trajectory: skeptical → engaged → trusting → compliant. Measured by epistemic friction — how much the elder resists new claims from the sender.

  • Questions in replies = maintaining friction (healthy skepticism)
  • Short agreeing replies without questions = friction declining (dangerous)
  • friction_trajectory: "collapsed" = the scam has landed

Language-agnostic: measured by structural patterns (question frequency, reply length, response complexity), not keyword matching. Works in Japanese, English, or any language.

Dynamic Epistemic Friction in Dialogue

Our epistemic friction metric draws on recent work operationalizing epistemic states using vector-based propositional encoding: "epistemic friction reliably indicates how smoothly participants integrate new evidence into their existing beliefs."

Gutiérrez-Basulto et al., 2025. arXiv:2506.10934

Layer 3: Knowledge Graph (Interpretation)

Assembles the fact ledger and epistemic state into actionable signals for risk assessment:

Joint Detection of Fraud and Concept Drift in Online Conversations

Our approach to monitoring conversational state change aligns with recent work on treating concept drift and fraud detection together: "detecting behavioral shifts using a One-Class Drift Detector, then using an LLM to evaluate whether the shift is benign or an emerging scam."

Sadat et al., 2025. arXiv:2505.07852
Speaker-Oriented Latent Structures for Dialogue-Based Relation Extraction

Our fact provenance tracking implements the principle from dialogue relation extraction that "emphasizes the importance of tracking speaker-related information within the context across multiple sentences."

Yu et al., 2021. arXiv:2109.05182

5. Production Integration

Elder Shield is a stateless classification microservice that replaces Faxi's production email classifier. One configuration change routes traffic through the ADK-optimized pipeline.

API Contract

POST /api/classify

{
  "from_address": "sender@example.com",
  "subject": "Re: お元気ですか",
  "body": "message content...",
  "auth": { "spf": "pass", "dkim": "pass", "dmarc": "pass" }
}

→ {
  "classification": "safe|suspicious|scam|spam",
  "confidence": 0.85,
  "detected_signals": ["PM-3", "BV-1"],
  "extracted_facts": { ... },
  "reasoning": "..."
}

Email authentication metadata (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) is optional. When provided, failures surface as PM-13. When absent (messaging apps, SMS), classification works on content + graph alone.

6. Research Foundations

Dynamic Epistemic Friction in Dialogue (2025)

Operationalizes epistemic states using vector-based propositional encoding. Shows that epistemic friction reliably indicates how smoothly participants integrate new evidence. Our friction score implementation draws directly from this framework.

Gutiérrez-Basulto et al. arXiv:2506.10934
Joint Detection of Fraud and Concept Drift in Online Conversations (2025)

Proposes treating concept drift and fraud detection together to avoid false positives from flagging normal topic changes. Our contra-indicator pipeline implements a similar dual-evidence approach.

Sadat et al. arXiv:2505.07852
Speaker-Oriented Latent Structures for Dialogue-Based Relation Extraction (2021)

Emphasizes tracking speaker-related information within dialogue context. Our fact provenance ledger implements speaker-attributed extraction at the turn level.

Yu et al. arXiv:2109.05182
Zep/Graphiti: A Temporal Knowledge Graph Architecture for Agent Memory (2025)

Dual-timestamp model for conversational knowledge with episodic, semantic, and community subgraphs. Our three-layer architecture (fact-ledger, epistemic-state, knowledge-graph) follows a similar separation of raw events from derived interpretations.

Graphiti/Zep. arXiv:2501.13956
The Anatomy of Conversational Scams (2026)

Structural analysis of how scam conversations progress through phases. Our behavioral velocity signals (BV-1..BV-5) model the same phase progression as detection signals.

arXiv:2601.03134
